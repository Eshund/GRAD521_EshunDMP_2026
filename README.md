Description of project
This is going to be a data management plan for GRAD521.

My research is in civil and construction engineering, with a focus on sustainable cement materials, specifically Limestone Calcined Clay Cement (LC3). LC3 is a low carbon alternative to conventional Portland cement because it replaces a large portion of clinker with calcined clay and limestone, which significantly reduces carbon dioxide emissions. However, although LC3 has strong environmental benefits, its long-term durability is not yet fully understood, especially under real exposure conditions. The main objective of my research is to understand how the internal pore structure of LC3 controls transport properties such as permeability, moisture movement, and carbonation. In simple terms, I am studying how water and gases move through the material and how this affects its durability over time. The key research questions are how pore connectivity influences transport behavior, how electrical resistivity can be used to quantify this pore structure, and how carbonation alters the material and affects its long-term performance.
